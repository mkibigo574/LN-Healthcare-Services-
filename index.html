Everything You Need to Know About the NDIS

Navigating the NDIS can feel overwhelming. We've put together a simple guide to help participants and families understand the scheme, eligibility, and what to expect.

What is the NDIS?

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is Australia's largest social support reform, providing funding to over 760,000 Australians with permanent and significant disabilities to access the supports they need to live an ordinary life.

1
Get Access

Apply through the NDIA to confirm eligibility.

2

3
Choose Provider

Connect with an NDIS registered provider like LN Health Care Services.

4
Receive Supports

Begin receiving the personalised supports outlined in your plan.

Am I Eligible for the NDIS?

To access the NDIS, you must meet four key criteria. Our team can help you understand each one and guide you through the process at no cost.

Age

You must be under 65 years old when you apply.

Residency

An Australian citizen, permanent resident, or Protected Special Category Visa holder.

Permanent Disability

A disability that significantly affects your everyday life.

Early Intervention

Or, you require early intervention support to reduce the impact of your disability.

How to Apply for the NDIS

The application process is straightforward, but we know it can feel daunting. Our team will support you through each step.

1
Contact NDIA

Call 1800 800 110 or apply online to start the process.

2
Complete ARF

Submit the Access Request Form with your details.

3
Gather Evidence

Collect medical reports and supporting documentation.

4
Assessment

NDIA reviews your application against access criteria.

5
Planning Meeting

Once approved, attend a planning meeting to develop your first plan.

Understanding Your First Plan

Your NDIS plan funding is divided into three categories, each designed to fund different types of supports based on your goals.

Core Supports

Daily activities, consumables, transport, and social participation.

Capacity Building

Building independence and skills — therapy, support coordination, training.

Capital

Higher-cost supports — assistive technology, home modifications, vehicles.

What is the NDIS and who is it for?

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is a federally funded program that provides support to Australians under 65 with a permanent and significant disability. It funds reasonable and necessary supports that help participants pursue their goals and live an ordinary life.

How do I become an NDIS participant?

You apply by completing an Access Request Form through the NDIA. We can guide you through this process step by step, including help gathering supporting evidence from your healthcare providers.

Can I switch from my current provider to LN Health Care Services?

Absolutely. As an NDIS participant, you have full choice and control. We make the transition seamless — simply contact us, and we'll handle the paperwork and ensure continuity of care.

What areas does LN Health Care Services service?

We currently operate across the Northern Territory (with our Darwin office) and Western Australia (Perth office), and surrounding regions. Contact us to confirm coverage for your specific area.

How are NDIS supports funded and managed?

NDIS plans can be NDIA-managed, plan-managed, or self-managed. We work with all three options. Funding is divided into Core, Capacity Building, and Capital categories — and we'll help you make the most of every dollar.

How do I refer someone to LN Health Care Services?

Use the Participant Referral Form on this page, email admin@lnhealthcareservices.com.au, or call our team directly. Referrals from family, support coordinators, healthcare professionals, and self-referrals are all welcome.

What qualifications do your support workers have?

All our staff hold current NDIS Worker Screening Checks, First Aid certifications, and most have Certificate III or IV in Individual Support / Disability. We provide ongoing training to maintain the highest quality standards.

How quickly can services begin after referral?

We aim to respond to all referrals within 2 business days. Depending on your needs and location, services can typically commence within 5–10 business days following an initial intake meeting.
